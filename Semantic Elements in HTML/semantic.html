Flowers are the reproductive organs of plants, appearing about 135-140 million years ago. They range from the tiny Wolffia globosa to the giant Rafflesia arnoldii, the world's largest flower. Flowers serve as food, medicine, and symbolic elements in culture, with unique characteristics like moving sunflower heads, chocolate-scented flowers, and carnivorous plants like the Venus flytrap.
